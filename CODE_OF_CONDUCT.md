# Code of Conduct

PatchArena contributors are expected to make collaboration safe, respectful, and technically
constructive.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful of differing experience, backgrounds, and viewpoints.
- Critique ideas and code with specific evidence, not people.
- Accept correction, acknowledge uncertainty, and avoid overstating results.
- Protect private information, credentials, unpublished findings, and repository data.
- Respect project scope, security boundaries, and maintainer decisions.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate intimidation, sustained
disruption, doxxing, or publishing another person's private information is not acceptable.
Knowingly submitting fabricated benchmark results or exposing secrets is also unacceptable.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently
ban participants when necessary to protect the project and community. Enforcement decisions
should consider context, impact, prior behavior, and the safety of affected people.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository maintainers through the hosting platform's
available private contact or reporting mechanism. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive
personal information. Reports will be handled as privately and consistently as practical; no fixed
response-time SLA is promised.

This policy applies in project repositories, issue trackers, review threads, discussions, and
other spaces where someone represents PatchArena.
